How Missed Calls Are Quietly Killing Your Plumbing Business

Here’s a question most plumbing business owners never think to ask: how many calls did I miss this week? Not how many voicemails — how many calls came in that nobody picked up?

The answer is almost always higher than you’d guess. And each one represents a customer who needed help, couldn’t reach you, and called someone else.

The Numbers Behind Missed Calls

Industry research consistently shows that the majority of callers who reach voicemail will hang up without leaving a message. For service businesses, that number is often above 60%. Think about what that means for a plumbing company that gets 10–15 calls a day: if you’re missing even 3–4 of those, and most don’t leave a voicemail, you could be losing 15–20 potential jobs a week without ever knowing it.

At an average job value of $300–$500, that’s $4,500–$10,000 in potential revenue disappearing every week. Over a year, you’re looking at a gap that could fund another truck, another technician, or a serious marketing push.

Why It Happens

It’s not negligence. It’s the nature of the job. Plumbers work with their hands. You can’t answer a phone when you’re soldering a pipe or snaking a drain. Even if you have someone in the office, they take lunch breaks, handle walk-ins, and can only answer one line at a time. After hours, weekends, and early mornings are completely uncovered unless you’re paying for overnight staff.

The callers don’t know any of this. All they know is nobody picked up, and the next plumber on Google is one tap away.

The Compounding Effect

Missed calls don’t just cost you one job. That customer you didn’t answer? They found another plumber who picked up, did a great job, and now they’re that plumber’s customer for every future call. They’ll recommend that plumber to their neighbors. You didn’t just lose a $400 drain cleaning — you lost a customer with a lifetime value of $5,000 or more.

It works in the other direction too. The business that answers every call builds a reputation for reliability. Customers remember who picked up when they were stressed about a leak at 7 PM. That trust compounds over years.

What You Can Do About It

The first step is actually measuring the problem. Check your phone system’s missed call log for the past month. Count the calls that went unanswered and never resulted in a voicemail. That number is your baseline.

From there, you have options. A traditional answering service will catch some of those calls but only takes messages — you still have to call everyone back. A dedicated office person helps during business hours but doesn’t cover evenings or weekends. An AI answering service covers every hour, every day, and actually books the appointment during the call so there’s no follow-up required.

Whatever you choose, the worst option is doing nothing. Every week you operate without full call coverage is another week of revenue you’ll never recover.
